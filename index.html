Agentic AI: Concepts & CVA Convictions

Definition

What is Agentic AI?

Understanding the paradigm shift from assistance to delegated execution — the foundation for everything that follows

🎯

Goal-Directed Agency

Autonomous agents don't just analyze or advise—they decide, execute multi-step tasks, and coordinate with tools and other agents to achieve objectives with minimal human intervention.

⚡

Beyond Automation

Unlike RPA or rule-based systems, agentic AI is adaptive and context-aware. Agents handle novel situations, adjust plans dynamically, and operate in environments with uncertainty.

🔗

Orchestrated Ecosystems

The most powerful applications involve multiple specialized agents collaborating—passing tasks, negotiating resources, working in parallel—coordinated by an orchestration layer.

Strategic Shift

Why "Agentic" Changes the Enterprise

Agentic AI shifts from "assistance" to delegated execution. Organizations must fundamentally redesign how work, decisions, and accountability operate.

This is not an IT transformation. It's an operating model transformation that touches strategy, governance, organization, and workforce simultaneously.

The CEO and ExCo cannot delegate this to "AI teams." These are strategic choices that will define competitive positioning for the next decade.

1

Work becomes a network of goal-driven workflows

Not sequential processes, but interconnected Decision-Action Loops (DALs) that can be delegated, monitored, and optimized as autonomous units.

2

Decision rights become the strategic bottleneck

The critical question: who (human or agent) is allowed to decide what, under which conditions, with what oversight?

3

Governance becomes an enabler, not a brake

Bounded autonomy + auditability transforms governance from friction into an accelerator for scale.

Clarification

DBAs and DALs: Two Lenses, One Method

DAWN® starts with a LEAD-style segmentation to define the competitive battlefield, then redesigns execution around Decision-Action Loops (DALs). DBAs help you prioritize; DALs are the unit of operating model design.

🏢

DBAs (or Domains)

Where you compete. If the company is multi-business, we reconstruct DBAs. If it is single-core, we use Domains (value streams, customer journeys, product lines, or enterprise functions). This is the strategic container for prioritization.

🔄

DALs

How work gets done. Recurring decision-and-action loops that can be redesigned, delegated to agents at a chosen autonomy level, and governed with explicit guardrails. DALs are the unit of design.

🔗

Linkages

Where sharing creates advantage. Shared data, decision logic, platforms, or controls that create a real cost/price impact—not just coordination overhead. Linkages amplify value across DALs.

M1.1Agentic Value Added Grid Construction▼

Define the real value chain, then enrich each cell with 5 agentic attributes rated 0–5:

  • Automatability (0–5): Potential for agent execution
  • Decision Criticality (0–5): Impact if decision is wrong
  • Data Moat (0–5): Quality, exclusivity, freshness
  • Trust Moat (0–5): Auditability, compliance needs
  • Disintermediation Risk (0–5): Exposure to bypass
📏 Scoring Rubric (0–5) — Keep it consistent across teams
  • Automatability: 0 = mostly bespoke/ambiguous; 3 = semi-structured with patterns; 5 = highly structured & repeatable
  • Decision criticality: 0 = reversible/low impact; 3 = moderate financial or customer impact; 5 = high-stakes/regulatory/irreversible
  • Data moat: 0 = commodity data; 3 = differentiated first-party data; 5 = unique, compounding data advantage
  • Trust moat: 0 = low trust requirement; 3 = moderate trust & explainability needed; 5 = high trust/safety/auditability required
  • Disintermediation risk: 0 = low risk; 3 = plausible platform encroachment; 5 = imminent risk of margin compression or loss of interface
M1.2DBA (or Domain) Reconstruction & Linkage Identification▼

Transform into Discrete Business Activities (DBAs) or Domains — the strategic containers (LEAD® heritage). If the company is multi-business, we reconstruct DBAs. If it is single-core, we use Domains (value streams, customer journeys, product lines, or enterprise functions).

  • Identify 8–20 DBAs (or domains) depending on complexity
  • Map "linkages" — shared data, processes, decision logic that create a real cost/price impact
  • Document coordination points where DALs interact across containers

M1.3DAL Portfolio Definition (2-Tier)▼

Extract 3–10 Decision-Action Loops per DBA (30–100 total), plus 10–20 Enterprise DALs that cut across DBAs (Finance, Risk, HR, Procurement). This prevents forcing "support" decisions into market DBAs while keeping a coherent portfolio logic.

We build a two-tier portfolio: a broad DAL Inventory (30–100 loops) for coverage, then a Priority DAL Deep-Dive (top 10–15) using full DIDA+ one-pagers to enable AOM design and lighthouse selection.

🎯 What qualifies as a DAL?
  • Recurring: daily/weekly/monthly cycle, not a one-off project decision
  • Actionable: triggers real actions across systems/teams
  • Measurable: clear KPIs and outcomes
  • Ownable: one accountable business owner
